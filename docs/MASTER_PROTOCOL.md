$$R = \frac{8 \eta L}{\pi r^4}$$

$$\text{MAP} \approx \text{CO} \times R \quad \implies \quad \text{CPP} = \text{MAP} - \text{ICP}$$

This sudden pressure drop triggers cerebral ischemia and transient syncope (passing out).

SECTION 4: SUBJECT ELIGIBILITY CRITERIA

4.1 Hepatic Function Inclusion Metrics

Screening laboratories must show baseline clearances within strict limits:

-   Serum Alanine Aminotransferase (ALT): $\le 1.5 \times \text{ULN}$
-   Serum Aspartate Aminotransferase (AST): $\le 1.5 \times \text{ULN}$
-   Total Bilirubin: $\le 1.2\text{ mg/dL}$
-   Serum Albumin: $\ge 3.5\text{ g/dL}$

4.2 Hepatic and Biliary Exclusion Targets

Candidates are immediately excluded if they present with:

-   History or current diagnostics of Cholelithiasis (gallstones) or localized Biliary Obstruction.
-   Active hepatic pathology (Hepatitis A/B/C, active cirrhosis Child-Pugh Class A-C, advanced NASH).
-   Documented history of acute Drug-Induced Liver Injury (DILI).

4.3 Concurrent Medication Exclusion Parameters

Due to potential piperine-driven CYP3A4 inhibition, subjects taking medications with narrow therapeutic windows, such as certain anticoagulants, antiplatelets, cardiovascular agents (e.g., Digoxin), immunosuppressants, or high-dose statins, are excluded.

4.4 Pre-Study Drug Washout Windows

A mandatory 5-half-life (\(t_{1/2}\)) washout period is required for any contraindicated medication to ensure subject safety.
